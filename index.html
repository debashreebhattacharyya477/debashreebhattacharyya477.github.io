HRMS & DIGITAL HR

End-to-end implementation of HRMS for a growing organisation

A mid-sized, fast-scaling organisation needed to move away from scattered spreadsheets and manual approvals into a single, reliable HR system.

What I did
  • Led requirements gathering across leaders, managers and HR to map “how work really happens”.
  • Designed workflows for core HR processes including onboarding, leave, travel and reimbursements.
  • Configured the HRMS, coordinated UAT and planned a phased rollout with clear ownership.
  • Created simple guides and training for managers and employees to adopt the new system.
Impact
  • Significantly reduced manual intervention and follow-ups for routine HR processes.
  • Improved accuracy and visibility of employee data for leaders and HR.
  • Gave managers a single, clear place to approve requests and track their teams.
Keywords: HRMS implementation, change management, process design, automation
GLOBAL HR OPERATIONS

Stabilising HR operations across multiple countries

A technology organisation with employees across countries needed clearer, more consistent HR operations and payroll practices without losing local nuances.

What I did
  • Mapped existing HR and payroll processes across locations to identify gaps and overlaps.
  • Standardised core HR workflows while allowing for local policy and legal differences.
  • Coordinated with external partners and internal teams to align cut-offs, approvals and data flows.
  • Set up simple documentation and checklists so day-to-day operations were less person-dependent.
Impact
  • Reduced confusion and errors in payroll inputs and HR operations across locations.
  • Improved predictability of HR processes for employees and managers.
  • Provided leadership with clearer visibility into headcount and people metrics.
Keywords: global HR, multi-country payroll, standardisation, operations
PERFORMANCE & TALENT

Refreshing performance management for a hybrid team

A growing team with mixed onsite and remote roles needed a performance approach that was structured but not heavy, and more aligned to how work was actually delivered.

What I did
  • Worked with leadership to define what “good performance” looks like in their context.
  • Introduced a simpler goal-setting and review rhythm with clear expectations for managers.
  • Equipped managers with conversation guides and templates for feedback discussions.
  • Built a lightweight process for documenting outcomes and linking them to rewards decisions.
Impact
  • More consistent performance conversations across teams and levels.
  • Clearer link between goals, feedback and decisions on growth or rewards.
  • Reduced resistance to the performance cycle because it felt usable and fair.
Keywords: performance management, manager enablement, hybrid teams
ONBOARDING & EMPLOYEE EXPERIENCE

Designing a structured onboarding journey

New hires were experiencing inconsistent onboarding, leading to confusion, delays and slower integration.

What I did
  • Mapped the ideal first 30–90 days for new hires across key roles.
  • Defined touchpoints for HR, managers and buddies, with clear ownership.
  • Created standard templates for welcome emails, checklists and feedback collection.
  • Aligned the onboarding flow with HR systems so most steps were trackable.
Impact
  • New hires felt clearer about expectations, people and processes.
  • Managers had a simple, repeatable structure instead of starting from scratch each time.
  • Faster time-to-productivity and fewer escalations around “who is doing what”.
Keywords: onboarding, employee experience, process mapping
HR ANALYTICS & INSIGHTS

Turning HR data into usable insights for leadership

Leadership wanted more than raw HR numbers—they needed patterns, risks and options.

What I did
  • Consolidated data on headcount, movement, performance and attrition into a simple view.
  • Identified trends and hotspots that required interventions or deeper conversations.
  • Presented insights in plain language, with recommended next steps for leaders and HR.
  • Built a basic rhythm for reviewing people metrics as part of business discussions.
Impact
  • Leaders could connect people metrics to business outcomes more easily.
  • HR conversations shifted from reactive firefighting to proactive planning.
  • Better prioritisation of where to focus limited HR time and resources.
Keywords: HR analytics, leadership reporting, decision support
